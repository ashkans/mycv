# Scoring Guide

## Salary Uplift vs CSIRO

- 5 = `90k+` base increase
- 4 = `60k-89k` base increase
- 3 = `40k-59k` base increase
- 2 = `20k-39k` base increase
- 1 = less than `20k` base increase

## Technical Depth

- 5 = strong modelling, engineering, and production ownership
- 3 = solid technical work but mixed with reporting/delivery
- 1 = mostly coordination, reporting, or consulting

## ML Relevance / Future Upside

- 5 = clear model-building / ML growth path
- 3 = adjacent to ML but not core
- 1 = little real ML opportunity

## Product / Commercial Exposure

- 5 = direct product impact, enterprise customers, commercial ownership
- 3 = some exposure, but limited ownership
- 1 = little product or business exposure

## Leadership / Career Growth

- 5 = strong path to senior technical/leadership growth
- 3 = modest title or scope increase
- 1 = unclear growth path

## Company Quality / Traction

- 5 = strong team, paying customers, credible traction
- 3 = promising but limited proof
- 1 = weak signals or unclear business

## Risk / Stability Fit

- 5 = startup risk feels acceptable for the upside
- 3 = moderate risk concerns
- 1 = risk too high for the reward

# Minimum Score To Accept

- `80+` = strong yes
- `70-79` = yes if compensation and scope are clearly better than CSIRO
- `60-69` = probably wait
- `<60` = decline

# Current Recommendation

- Do not decide before learning:
  - exact salary range
  - Senior vs Lead scope
  - how much real ML/model building is involved
  - company traction and manager/team quality

- Provisional threshold:
  - Accept only if the role scores at least `75/100`
  - If ML relevance is `2/5` or lower, require a much stronger salary uplift to justify the move
